Press Release from ORG: ORGORG ("ORG"), a subsidiary of ORG, has agreed to acquire ORG, the self-driving division of ORG, one of the largest ride-hailing companies in the world. This marks the first major deal for the newly established ORG.the first major deal for the newly established ORG will bring together world-class scientists and software engineers from ORG, ORG, and researchers from ORG ("ORG") already working together with ORG. The resulting combined "dream team" of approximately 1,200 will represent one of the most diverse, well-resourced and talented groups in this field.Today's announcement places ORG at the center of the connected and automated driving movement by providing:-- Talent: an international team of top-tier engineers, research scientists, and domain experts in mobility services.-- Technology: a robust set of sensing, computing, and software assets, and strategic capabilities in automated driving systems-- International Footprint: once the acquisition is complete, ORG will have an expanded footprint beyond ORG Tokyo headquarters, with offices and engineering teams in Palo Alto, CA and London, UK.In addition to the acquisition of ORG, ORG and ORG have signed commercial agreements for utilization of the ORG system and fleet data to accelerate the safety and commercialization of the automated-driving technology that ORG will develop.ORG will receive approximately $550 million in cash, with $200 million paid upfront subject to certain closing adjustments and $350 million of payments over a five-year period.As a result of this transaction, ORG will accelerate revolutionizing the world of mobility and realize ORG mission of "Mobility to Love, Safety to Live," fueling a broader vision of human happiness and well-being on the planet Earth.Comment from James Kuffner, CEO, ORG"This acquisition advances our mission to develop the safest mobility in the world at scale.The ORG team, alongside the team of researchers at ORG, have already established a center of excellence for software development and technology in the ORG Group.
