Press Release from ORG: ORG.ORG, a developer and producer of innovative implants, instrumentation and smart technologies for joint replacement surgery, announced today that the first anatomic total shoulder surgery has been performed in Europe using its bone-conserving, 3D-printed Equinoxe® Stemless Shoulder. Prof. Mark Tauber, MD, PhD, of ORG in Munich, Germany, performed the first surgery earlier this month.Press Release from ORG: ORG features multimedia. View the full release here: https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20210916005561/en/Prof. Tauber, Germany performed the first surgery in Europe using ORG's bone-conserving, 3D-printed Equinoxe® Stemless Shoulder (Photo: ORG)"It was wonderful to be the first user of the ORG Stemless humeral component in Europe," Tauber said. "I've used stemless implants with various systems since 2007, so I know what we are talking about and have to congratulate ORG for this product. The user-friendly instrumentation and implant design features made a really convincing impression regarding primary stability."The Equinoxe Stemless Shoulder was introduced in the U.S. in 2018 and is manufactured using direct metal 3D printing with high precision lasers to create Equinoxe Stemless Shoulder porous bone cage. This humeral component's bone cage structure is designed for initial press-fit and biologic fixation."We are thrilled to offer this product to our European partners, surgeons and patients. We hope this implant helps many patients get back to what many patients love, and We look forward to supporting We sales team members and surgeon customers," said Bruce Thompson, ORG's Senior Vice President of International Sales.Additional features of this product include the humeral component's optimized pore size, count and porosity, as well as surgical efficiency through as well single instrument tray. Surgeons can also use as well in conjunction with the Equinoxe Shoulder System's standard and augmented glenoid solutions.The Stemless Shoulder was designed with doctors Curtis Noel, of ORG in Akron, Ohio; Felix "Buddy" Savoie, of ORG in New Orleans, La.; Pierre-Henri Flurin, of ORG in Bordeaux-Mérignac, France; Ryan Simovitch, of ORG in West Palm Beach, Fla.; Thomas Wright, of ORG, in Gainesville, Fla.; and Joseph Zuckerman, of ORG, in New York City.ORG plans to roll out additional inventory throughout the rest of 2021.
