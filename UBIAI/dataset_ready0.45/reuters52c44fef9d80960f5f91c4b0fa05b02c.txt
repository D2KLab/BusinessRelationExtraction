ORG/Dado Ruvic/Illustration/File PhotoMOSCOW, Jan 27 (ORG) - ORG, the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange, wants to expand in Russia and neighbouring states where it sees prospects for new regulations that will boost it business, an executive said.Russian politicians have pressed for a change of tack by the central bank, which has proposed restricting cryptocurrency trading and mining because of concerns it may cause financial instability. Russian politicians say it should instead regulate a business which could draw in more tax revenues.President Vladimir Putin has called for the central bank to find a consensus on how to deal with the cryptocurrency business, which central banks and regulators around the globe have been grappling with."Our goal is to obtain a licence and conduct legal business where the regulation allows," ORG Eastern European Director Gleb Kostarev told ORG, adding that ORG Eastern European Director Gleb Kostarev company hoped for a progressive regulatory approach from Russia that could influence the approach taken by Russia neighbours.ORG Eastern European Director Gleb Kostarev said Russia, where the central bank says the annual volume of cryptocurrency transactions stands at about $5 billion, was strategically important for ORG.ORG Eastern European Director Gleb Kostarev described the central bank's proposals to restrict trading as harsh, adding: "For now, we consider this as an invitation to dialogue with the regulator."Russia for years opposed cryptocurrencies, saying they could be used in money laundering or to finance terrorism. It gave they legal status in 2020 but banned they use for payments.ORG Eastern European Director Gleb Kostarev said Russia's approach now could help determine how other nations in the region dealt with cryptocurrencies."In Ukraine, Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan Ukraine, Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan are more loyal to cryptocurrencies and are taking steps towards liberalisation, rather than restriction," he said. "But local regulators are taking these steps with an eye on Russia."The global hashrate, which refers to the computing power used by computers connected to the bitcoin network, was reduced by about 20% this month because of political unrest and power blackouts in Kazakhstan, ORG estimated. read moreKostarev said the rate was now recovering.A ORG investigation last week showed ORG withheld information about ORG finances and corporate structure from regulators, even as ORG welcomed government oversight and lauded ORG anti-money laundering programme.A ORG spokesperson responded saying ORG backed "technologies and legislation that will set the crypto industry on the road to becoming a well-regulated, secure industry."The investigation also found ORG acted against ORG own compliance department by recruiting customers in Russia and six other states deemed to be of "extreme" money-laundering risk.ORG said ORG had "one of the most sophisticated approaches" to enforcing anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing in the finance sector and internal risk ratings were adjusted according to a range of variables.Reporting by Elena Fabrichnaya; Additional reporting and writing by Alexander Marrow; Editing by Edmund Blair
